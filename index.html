Smart Recipe Design

A case study by

Anika McPhee (UX Designer)

Faye Teodoro (UX Designer)

Vaishali Singh (UX Designer)

Conducted April-May 2023

The Design Challenge

Significant number of people struggle with finding new and exciting recipes that cater to their food preferences

The Solution

Creating AI-powered mobile application that generates personalized recipes for users based on their dietary preferences, available ingredients, and desired meal types.

Research Question

"What are the needs, preferences, and challenges users face about recipe discovery and meal planning?"

Research Objectives

Analyze user behavior's and challenges in meal planning.

Identify user preferences and dietary restrictions

Assess user satisfaction with existing recipe and meal-planning apps

User Research

The Plan

Determine: "What are the needs, preferences, and challenges users face about recipe discovery and meal planning?"

Set up online surveys and engage in semi-structured interviews to achieve a balance between qualitative and quantitative research. Zoom will be used for the interviews, and Google Form will be used for the survey.

Participant Interviews

Five remote interviews were conducted throughout April 2023. Seventeen qualitative interview questions were targeted to provide insight into our research questions. The duration was approximately half an hour. Participants consented to being recorded for research purposes. To view recordings transcripts, click here.

Affinity Diagram Tecnique

We plotted out all our collective data from the user interviews and online survey results. 66.7% of users experience pain points related to the length of time healthy meal preparation takes and sited that as a reason meal planning does not occur. 33.3% of users struggled with finding healthy meal options for their unique dietary restrictions. .

Proto Persona

Meet Richard Robinson, our user persona, who was created based on the data from the affinity diagram and empathy map. Richard is a 30 year old financial advisor (workaholic) from Toronto, Ontario, Canada. He’s currently single and working long hours at the office. He takes his job seriously to the point where his healthy food choices have taken a back seat. He hopes to improve his knowledge about food, nutrition and cooking techniques. He wants to find new recipes to impress his friends and family with. He’d like to maintain a healthy lifestyle despite his busy work schedule. A bonus would be if he could save money by utilizing the ingrediency in his pantry. He often finds that he doesn’t know what to do with what’s in his cupboards before the items expire and is wasted. Some of his Pain-points include: -How-To videos are often far-too-long. -He’s always missing one or two things that he needs. -Meal preparation is time consuming and he has a busy schedule.

Define and Ideate

User Insight Statement

Young health-conscious professional workers need to efficiently plan diverse and nutritious meals for themselves because their busy schedules often lead to repetitive and less healthy meal choices which negatively impact their overall well-being.

Problem Statement

We have observed through user research that a busy schedule increases the challenges of healthy meal planning. Users are less likely to watch “how-to videos” or try recipes that require an investment of time. Users need an easy and fast way to put together healthy recipes. How might we develop a recipe app that is convenient and time efficient for users? Success will be measured based on the decreased time it takes to prepare meals.

Design

User Flow

We then dove into designing the user flow. The initial flow was the onboarding process. We mapped out how we would guide users and recreated the path that the user would take for signing up. Because the app focuses on healthy recipes, we leaned into capturing user food preferences as it was a common pain-point. After the completion of the sign-up flow, a separate on-boarding flow was designed for the health aspect. We gave the user the ability to skip the process and go through the flow and enter information on their terms and time.

Test

Usability Study

Next, we prepared for moderated usability testing. For our test, we developed four tasks for our participants to complete. Our goal was to determine if the hierarchy and organization of information makes sense and if the participants were capable of preforming each task.

Results

Turns out, users had a difficult time understanding how the nutrition aspect was connected to the healthy recipes aspect. We knew that our design required further definition and a clearer purpose. As a result, we changed the design and removed the nutrition flow entirely. This focused the purpose on the initial goal, creating an app that provides quick healthy recipe options for busy individuals.
